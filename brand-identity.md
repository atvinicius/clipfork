## 1. NAME

### Competitive Landscape Context

Before naming, here is how the competitors position themselves:

| Competitor | Positioning | Key Angle |
|---|---|---|
| **Creatify** | "The AI Ad Generator" | URL-to-video, 500+ avatars, batch mode |
| **Arcads** | "#1 Platform to Create AI UGC" | Layer-based editor, voice tuning |
| **MakeUGC** | "#1 Platform to Create AI UGC" | Script-to-video, 3.1x avg ROAS |
| **Bluma** | "AI Short-Form Content Engine" | Clone winning videos, de-editing, auto-posting |
| **ReelFarm** | "AI TikTok Automation" | Slideshow-first, auto-publish to TikTok |

**Key gap:** None of these names directly evoke *speed*, *cloning*, or *viral replication*. They feel either generic ("MakeUGC") or abstract ("Bluma", "Arcads"). There is a clear naming opportunity around the core mechanic: forking/cloning viral content structures.

---

### Name Options (Ranked)

#### TOP RECOMMENDATION: **ClipFork**
- **Why it works:** "Clip" = short-form video. "Fork" = developer-native term for cloning/replicating something and making it your own (like forking a repo). It is precise about the product's core action. Modern, slightly technical, instantly understandable to the target audience (marketers who work with dev teams and understand tech culture).
- **Characters:** 8
- **Domain signals:** clipfork.com and clipfork.ai return zero results on web searches -- no existing company, product, or project uses this name. High probability of availability.
- **Trademark risk:** Low. No existing companies in adjacent spaces.

#### Option 2: **SpawnVid**
- **Why it works:** "Spawn" = to generate, to create copies rapidly. "Vid" = video. Feels fast, generative, slightly aggressive. Evokes rapid creation.
- **Characters:** 8
- **Domain signals:** spawnvid.com and spawnvid.ai return zero search results. No existing product. "Spawn" as a standalone brand is taken (spawn.co -- a game builder), but "SpawnVid" is clear.
- **Risk:** "Spawn" has minor dark/gaming connotations (the comic character). Could feel slightly aggressive for some brands.

#### Option 3: **Rekclip**
- **Why it works:** A compressed play on "replicate" + "clip." Sounds like "wreck clip" which has an edgy, disruptive energy. Memorable, unusual.
- **Characters:** 7
- **Domain signals:** Zero search results for rekclip.com or rekclip.ai. Appears completely unregistered.
- **Risk:** Pronunciation ambiguity ("rek-clip" vs "re-clip"). Requires more brand education.

#### Option 4: **LoopVid**
- **Why it works:** "Loop" evokes virality (content going viral = looping through feeds). "Vid" is clear. Clean, friendly, approachable.
- **Characters:** 7
- **Domain signals:** loopvid.com/loopvid.ai return zero results. Clean namespace.
- **Risk:** Feels slightly tamer. "Loop" could be confused with looping video (GIFs). Less differentiated.

---

**Final recommendation: ClipFork.**

It is the most defensible, the most descriptive of the core product mechanic, and it bridges the tech-native audience (agencies, growth teams) with the creative use case. "Fork this viral clip" is a sentence that immediately explains the product.

## 3. BRAND COLORS

### Design philosophy
The visual system needs to feel like a *developer tool for creative people* -- precise, dark, fast -- but with electric energy that signals content creation and virality.

### Primary Palette

| Role | Name | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Primary** | Electric Violet | `#7C3AED` | CTAs, primary buttons, brand mark, key UI accents |
| **Primary Dark** | Deep Indigo | `#1E1B4B` | Sidebar background, navigation, dark surfaces |
| **Background** | Snow | `#FAFAFA` | Content area, cards, light surfaces |

### Secondary Palette

| Role | Name | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Secondary** | Neon Lime | `#A3E635` | Success states, accent highlights, "viral" indicators, hover states |
| **Secondary Dark** | Charcoal | `#18181B` | Text, headings, high-contrast elements |
| **Neutral** | Slate | `#64748B` | Body text, secondary labels, borders |

### Accent / Signal Colors

| Role | Name | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Accent Hot** | Coral Pink | `#F43F5E` | Notifications, trending indicators, urgency |
| **Accent Cool** | Cyan | `#06B6D4` | Links, information states, secondary interactive |
| **Surface** | Soft Violet | `#EDE9FE` | Hover backgrounds, selected states, tags |

### How it works together
- **Dark sidebar** (`#1E1B4B`) with Electric Violet active states and Snow-white text
- **Light content area** (`#FAFAFA`) with Charcoal text and Violet/Lime interactive elements
- **CTA buttons** use Electric Violet (`#7C3AED`) with white text; secondary buttons use Neon Lime on dark
- **Viral/trending indicators** use Coral Pink pulses
- The violet-to-lime gradient (`#7C3AED` to `#A3E635`) is the signature brand gradient, used sparingly on hero sections and marketing materials

**Why not blue?** Every SaaS platform defaults to blue. This violet-lime combination feels like the intersection of creative energy (violet) and growth/money (lime). It is distinctive in the AI UGC space where competitors use: blue (Creatify), purple-pink (Arcads), teal (MakeUGC), and orange (ReelFarm).

## 4. LOGO CONCEPT

### Primary Mark: The Fork Icon

**Concept:** A stylized play/video triangle that has been "forked" -- split into two diverging paths, like a Git fork diagram or a tuning fork.

**Detailed description:**
- Start with a classic video play button (right-pointing triangle)
- The right edge of the triangle splits into two parallel angled lines that diverge outward -- like a fork in a road, or the prongs of a tuning fork
- The split creates a subtle sense of multiplication/replication
- The icon is geometric, minimal, and works at 16px favicon size
- Rendered in Electric Violet (`#7C3AED`) as the solid fill, with the fork split lines in Neon Lime (`#A3E635`) to create contrast

**Wordmark:**
- "Clip" in medium weight, "Fork" in bold weight
- Font: Inter or a geometric sans-serif with similar DNA
- Tracking: tight (-0.02em)
- The "F" in Fork can optionally use a custom glyph where the horizontal strokes of the F angle slightly upward, echoing the fork motif

**Lockups:**
1. Icon + wordmark (horizontal) -- primary
2. Icon only -- app icon, favicon, social avatars
3. Wordmark only -- when the icon is too small to read

**Color variations:**
- Violet icon + dark wordmark on light backgrounds
- White icon + white wordmark on dark/violet backgrounds
- Single-color (all white or all dark) for constrained contexts

### FAQ SECTION

**Section Headline:**
## Questions? We have answers.

---

**Q: Is this legal? Am I copying someone else's content?**
A: You are not copying content. ClipFork analyzes the *structure* of a viral video -- the hook timing, pacing, transitions, and CTA placement -- not the actual footage, audio, or script. You rebuild the structure with your own original assets. This is the same thing every ad creative team does manually when they study competitor ads. ClipFork just automates the analysis.

**Q: How is ClipFork different from Creatify, Arcads, or MakeUGC?**
A: Those tools generate AI UGC videos from scratch or from product URLs. ClipFork starts from *proven viral structures*. Instead of hoping your AI-generated video works, you are rebuilding formats that already went viral. It is the difference between writing a song from scratch and studying the structure of a hit song before writing yours.

**Q: What video URLs can I fork?**
A: Any public TikTok or Instagram Reels URL. We are adding YouTube Shorts, Twitter/X video, and direct ad library URLs soon. You can also fork your *own* best-performing videos to create fresh variants.

**Q: How realistic are the AI avatars?**
A: Very. Our avatars support natural lip-sync, varied facial expressions, and realistic body movement. Most viewers cannot distinguish them from real UGC creators. You can also create a custom avatar from a 2-minute video of a real person (with their consent).

**Q: Can I use ClipFork for organic content, not just ads?**
A: Absolutely. Fork viral organic content structures to build your TikTok or Instagram presence. Many of our users fork trending formats for organic posting and then boost the winners as paid ads.

**Q: Do I need design or video editing skills?**
A: No. ClipFork handles the structural analysis, asset placement, timing, and export automatically. If you can paste a URL and upload a product photo, you can use ClipFork. For power users, we offer fine-grained controls over every aspect of the output.

**Q: What if the forked video does not perform well?**
A: That is what batch variants are for. Generate 10-50 variations of the same viral structure with different hooks, CTAs, and avatars. Test them in your ad account and let the data pick the winner. The structure is proven -- the variant testing finds the right execution for *your* audience.

**Q: Can my team collaborate on ClipFork?**
A: Yes. Growth and Scale plans include team workspaces with role-based access, shared template libraries, and approval workflows. Agency teams can manage multiple brand accounts from a single dashboard.
